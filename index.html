"Eol" is a uniquely Korean concept that
doesn't have a direct equivalent in
English. It refers to the innate spirit,
soul, or essential character of a person,
community, or culture. This term
embodies the idea of a deep, intrinsic
force that defines the core identity and
resilience of individuals or groups.
In the context of Korean culture, "eol" is
often associated with qualities such as
integrity, perseverance, and a profound
sense of purpose. It is considered a
fundamental element that shapes one's
actions, values, and life path. The
concept of "eol" also extends to the
collective consciousness, representing
the shared spirit and cultural identity of
the Korean people.
Overall, "eol" encapsulates a rich and
multifaceted understanding of the
human spirit, emphasizing moral
fortitude, cultural pride, and an
unwavering commitment to one's
principles and heritage.

전시개요

《얼:[əl] in NewYork》은 타지에서 외국인으로서 활동하며 정체성을 표현하는 다양한 방식을 보여주는 프로젝트의 첫번째로, 뉴욕에서 활동 중인 구지회, 문예지, 허정 작가와 함께한다. 이들은 외지에서 새로운 경험을 하며 쌓은 시간 하나하나로 자신의 정체성을 견고하게 만들고 있다.

‘얼’ 은 한국의 고유한 단어로 정신의 줏대를 의미한다. 타지에서 자신만의 정체성을 구축해가는 이들의 얼을 관객들에게 선보이며 전시에 참여하는 모든 이들은 이 전시를 통해 연결 고리를 만들게 된다. 본 전시 는 온라인 전시로 진행된다. 이 전시를 통해 관람자들에게 자신만의 ‘얼’에 대해 생각하고 소통하는 기회를 제공하고자 한다.



《얼: [əl] in New York》 is the first project in a series that shows various ways of expressing identity as a foreigner living abroad. It features Jihoe Koo, Yeji Moon, and Jeong Heo, who are artists in New York. These individuals are solidifying their identities through every new experience they gain while living away from their home country.

The term ‘얼’ is a indigenous Korean word meaning the essence of one's spirit or resolve. By presenting the ‘얼’ of those who are building their identities in a foreign land, this exhibition aims to create a connection between the participants and the audience. The exhibition will be held online, providing viewers with the opportunity to reflect on and engage with their own '얼.'
